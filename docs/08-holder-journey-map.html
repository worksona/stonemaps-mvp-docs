Stonemaps · Holder Journey

From first scan to lifelong follower — Field-Test MVP
1

Encounter

A stranger scans the QR on a stone and meets something that claims to talk.

Doing
  • Scans the QR; meets name, place, traits
  • Reads “this is a field test”
  • Taps Talk to the stone
Thinking

“A talking stone? A map, a voice — something else?”

Touchpoints
  • Stone + QRphysical object The artifact itself — QR printed on the stone resolves to its permanent URL
  • Landing/s/:stoneId Identity, traits, field-test frame, one CTA; static-first so it paints on cell networks
Leaves behind
◦device person_ref →QR → /s/:id ⇄pairing opens · person↔stone
Field-test learning
  • Scan → talk rate — does the landing convert curiosity?
  • Do people read below the fold before tapping?
Tech reqs
  • static/SSR landing, <1s first paint
  • opaque permanent stone ids
  • current-holder check on deeper routes
Where it breaks
  • Slow scan-to-paint kills the magic
  • URLs leak via photos — strangers land mid-tenure
The design answer
  • P5 — QR encodes canonical URL, static-first
  • Bystander view: “with someone right now”
Flow context
AFirst touch — begins here: QR scan → Landing. Anonymous, under a minute end to end; the nav stays locked until the first exchange.
UI built bystander view · open needs — · dec P5
Flow AStranger · anonymous
2

Threshold

The gate, crossed once: a gift left by the previous holder, and plain-language consent.

Doing
  • Receives the previous holder’s gift
  • Learns the stone keeps a quiet journal (opt-out)
  • Consent choice: share conversations with team — default off
Thinking

“Someone left this behind — for whoever came next. That’s me.”

Touchpoints
  • Welcome/s/:id/welcome Latest gift, framed as an inheritance; link to the full chain
  • PE Intro/s/:id/intro “What am I?” framing + the two disclosure rows + Terms/FAQ
  • The Gifts/s/:id/gifts Chain of every parting gift, oldest to newest — the stone’s memory made visible
Leaves behind
◦consent flags on pairing ◦gate-crossed flag →gate → nav unlocked ⇄inheritance · holder n−1 → n
Field-test learning
  • Gate drop-off — where do people abandon the two-step gate?
  • Team-share opt-in rate at default-off
Tech reqs
  • consent flags stored on the pairing
  • gifts-chain read model (ordered by pairing)
  • first-time-gate flag per pairing/device
Where it breaks
  • Consent stack reads as legalese — tone collapses
  • First circulation: no gift to show
The design answer
  • Disclosures split informational vs consent
  • First-circulation opening copy — logged gap
Flow context
AFirst touch — continues: Welcome → PE Intro form the once-per-pairing gate. Returning holders skip this entirely.
UI built gifts chain · to design needs M1 · M3
Flow ANew holder · anonymous
3

Converse

Private exchange with the Emissary — it opens from what the last holder left behind.

Doing
  • Talks with the Emissary; it opens from the last gift
  • Returns days later — straight to chat, history intact
  • Peeks at the PE Journal; may opt out
Thinking

“It doesn’t push. It sits with what’s carried.”

Touchpoints
  • PE Conversation/s/:id/emissary Chat with persona header (name, trait, rename); text, mic, attach
  • PE Journal/s/:id/emissary/journal The stone’s dated notes on this tenure; per-stone opt-out checkbox
  • Device storeIndexedDB Transcript lives here only — the privacy promise, made physical
Leaves behind
◦transcript · device only ✦PE journal entries →return path → chat ⇄thread · holder↔emissary
Field-test learning
  • Return rate mid-tenure & conversation depth
  • PE-journal opt-out rate — is the journal welcome?
Tech reqs
  • LLM endpoint w/ per-stone persona prompt
  • opening generated from previous gift
  • server-side PE-journal generation (skippable)
  • no server persistence of transcript (R1)
Where it breaks
  • One silent upload ends the privacy promise — and the product
  • iOS eviction can erase a browser-tab transcript
The design answer
  • R1 — transcript never leaves the device unconsented
  • P4 — re-bind prompt; Emissary degrades gracefully
Flow context
AFirst touch — ends here, at the first exchange with the Emissary; the nav unlocks.
BReturn, holding — reopen mid-tenure: straight to the chat, history intact, no gate.
UI built rename/edit · to design needs M4 · dec F1
Flows A · BHolder · anonymous
4

Participate

The shared spaces: what other holders left, and what this one adds to the chain.

Doing
  • Browses the Commons — Stone ⇄ Public feeds
  • Contributes; spends a one-time reply slot
  • Writes My Journal entries with share scopes
Thinking

“Others have passed through here before me.”

Touchpoints
  • Commons/s/:id/commons Card feed; toggle scopes; cards expand in place, reply slot inside
  • Contribute/s/:id/commons/new Compose + files; publish-to checkboxes: Stone and/or Public (≥1)
  • My Journal/s/:id/journal Private by default; per-entry shares: Stone / Commons / Team / PE-soon
Leaves behind
✦commons post ✦reply · slot spent ✦journal entry ◦scope choices →stone feed ⇄ public feed ⇄reply link · holder↔holder
Field-test learning
  • Contribution rate per holder; reply-slot usage
  • Which share scopes do people actually choose? (feeds F8 call)
Tech reqs
  • posts w/ dual scopes {stone, public}
  • reply slot: OPEN→CLOSED, terminal
  • media upload, client-side downscale
  • attribution by holder ordinal (R3)
Where it breaks
  • Names in shared spaces break the anonymity the tone needs
  • Scope confusion — posts land where unintended
The design answer
  • R3 — ordinal attribution, never identity
  • Scope matrix fixed; subtle scope marker on cards
Flow context
CCommons — read Stone ⇄ Public feeds, contribute, spend a one-time reply slot. My Journal rides alongside as the F8 module.
UI built expanded card · to design F8 my journal · open needs M2
Flow C · F8Holder · anonymous
5

Gift

The handoff — and the one ask. Leave something for the next holder; decide whether to keep following.

Doing
  • Composes a parting gift; confirms the handoff
  • Asked once: “want to see where it goes?” — email, or let it go
  • Stone resets for its next holder
Thinking

“This ends our conversation. I’d like to see where it goes.”

Touchpoints
  • Gift the Stone/s/:id/gift Compose + files; confirm dialog — “this ends your conversation”
  • Keep FollowingA1 · in-flow One email field, one skip; states what’s kept: chain, commons, archive
  • Claim emailtransactional Single button; 7-day single-use token; day-6 reminder (T6)
Leaves behind
✦parting gift → chain ◦pairing sealed · ordinal n ◦claim token · 7d →stone hop · n → n+1 ⇄chain link · giver → next ⇄email ↔ pairing · pending
Field-test learning
  • Claim rate at gifting — target ≥40%; the account layer’s whole argument
  • Skip reasons — is the ask trusted?
Tech reqs
  • pairing-end + stone-reset event
  • claim token issue: single-use, 7d TTL
  • transactional email sender class
  • gift text commits before media upload (P7)
Where it breaks
  • The ask reads as marketing — distrust at the emotional peak
  • Email captured after reset = the giver is already gone
The design answer
  • D2 — ask inside the gift flow, pre-reset, one-tap skip
  • Gifting never blocked on auth
Flow context
DGift — parting gift joins the chain → “keep following?” ask → stone resets. The claim offer lives inside this flow, pre-reset (D2).

Flow DGiver · the claim
6

Follow

The afterlife: the stone’s life goes on, and its past holder keeps a quiet line of sight.

Doing
  • Taps the magic link — lands on My Stones
  • Watches new gifts join the chain; reads stone commons
  • Revisits the archive; hears only when something real happens
Thinking

“A new holder left a gift today.”

Touchpoints
  • EmailT1 · T2 · T3 New gift / your reply / weekly digest; 1-a-day cap; deep-linked
  • My Stones/me Card per pairing: image, held-dates, activity dot
  • Afterlife/me/pairings/:id Gifts since your tenure, stone commons (read-only), provenance
  • Archive…/archive Your gift, contributions, journal entries, PE journal of your tenure
Leaves behind
✦account ✦archive · assembled ◦session · 90d sliding ◦seen cursor →email → afterlife deep link ⇄pairing ↔ account · claimed ⇄account ↔ stone · follows
Field-test learning
  • Email → deep-link rate; return visits per account/month
  • Does read-only feel right, or do past holders want a voice? (tests D6)
Tech reqs
  • magic-link verify; HttpOnly 90d sliding session (P3)
  • notification classes + prefs + unsubscribe
  • SEEN_CURSOR per pairing (activity dot)
  • works fully in plain browser (P1)
Where it breaks
  • Magic link opens in browser, not installed PWA — split session
  • Notification noise betrays the register
The design answer
  • P1 · P2 — browser-first afterlife; app re-requests a link
  • Anti-triggers + cap; afterlife read-only (D6)
Flow context
EClaim — magic link verifies email; the pairing attaches to an account.
FAfterlife — My Stones → new gifts, stone commons (read-only), own archive.
GNotifications — event-driven email only; opt-in, capped, deep-linked.
